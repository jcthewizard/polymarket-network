How Correlations Are Calculated

This visualization shows relationships between Polymarket prediction markets using log-return Pearson correlation. Markets that move together at the same time are connected.

The Algorithm

1

Fetch Price History

Get historical prices for each market from Polymarket's CLOB API

2

Align by Timestamp

Only use data points where both markets have values at the same time

3

Calculate Log Returns

Convert prices to returns: ln(price[i] / price[i-1])

4

Compute Pearson Correlation

Measure linear relationship between the two return series

5

Create Link if |r| > 0.5

Markets with correlation above 50% are connected in the visualization

Why Log Returns?

Raw price correlation can be misleading. If two markets both trend from 30% → 70% probability, they'll show high correlation even if their daily movements aren't synchronized.

Log returns capture whether markets move at the same time — this is the standard approach in quantitative finance.

Filters Applied

Volume Filter

Only markets with >$100,000 trading volume

Probability Filter

Only markets between 5% and 95% probability

Variance Filter

Skip stagnant markets with little price movement

Minimum Data Points

At least 10 aligned timestamps required

Pearson Correlation Formula

r = (n∑xy - ∑x∑y) / √[(n∑x² - (∑x)²)(n∑y² - (∑y)²)]

+1.0 = Perfect positive 0 = No relationship -1.0 = Perfect inverse

Data Source

Market data is fetched from the Polymarket CLOB API:

GET https://clob.polymarket.com/prices-history?market={clobTokenId}&interval=1d&fidelity=60

Data refreshes automatically every 10 minutes. Markets are classified using GPT-4o-mini.

Known Limitations

⚠️

Linear only: Pearson only captures linear relationships, not threshold effects

⚠️

Same-event variants: Sub-markets of the same event may correlate mechanically

⚠️

No lag detection: If Market A leads Market B by 1-2 days, this isn't captured
